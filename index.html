About This Course

Applied Machine Learning empowers complex systems to learn from data and make reliable decisions at scale. This course covers the complete ML pipeline from probabilistic foundations to state-of-the-art LLM training and deployment.

üéØ Key Learning Areas

  • Statistical Learning & Optimization
  • Feature Engineering & Selection
  • Model Evaluation & Validation
  • Neural Networks & Transformers
  • LLM Training & Deployment
  • Agentic Workflows & Tool Integration

üß© Prerequisites

  • Strong foundation in Python3, Data Structures, Linear Algebra, and Probability & Statistics
  • Experience with numpy, pandas, and pytorch is expected

  • 1 Final LLM Project: Team of ~5, includes presentation + 10-min video + project report

üìú Policies

Late-work policy

  • Work will be accepted with a 10% penalty for up to 24 hours past the original due date & time
  • Work will NOT be accepted more than 24 hours past the original due date & time

Re-grade policy

Students may request a re-grade within 1 week from the date the graded assignment is returned to students. If requesting a regrade, students should be prepared to make a compelling argument as to why the original grade needs to be reconsidered. The student should discuss the grade with the TA first before requesting instructor re-grade. The process for requesting an instructor re-grade is to email the instructor with the request within one week from the date the graded assignment is returned. The instructor and student will then decide on a mutually agreeable time and place to discuss the assignment

Use of Generative AI

AI is everywhere now, and thus assignments/projects in this course will permit/encourage the use of AI tools, such as ChatGPT and Gemini. In the assignments/projects, AI use must be appropriately acknowledged and cited. For instance, if you generated a whole paragraph through ChatGPT and edited it for accuracy, your submitted work would need to include a note such as ‚ÄúI generated this work through Chat GPT and edited the content for accuracy‚Äù. It is each student‚Äôs responsibility to assess the validity and applicability of any AI output that is submitted
